From - Eastern Branch Hana Association Clerical Staff
To - [CENSORED FOR PRIVACY] of the Tri-Axe Office


Good day, [CENSORED], Thank you for waiting, [CENSORED] I’m sure you’ve been very concerned about one of your fixers. Upon your request, we have found here a personal letter written by “Holden H.V”,  to “J.G.”, found at the personal residence of [CENSORED] of T. Corp Collections Office. I have personally transcripted it, and footnoted certain parts, for your further information. Note: his colloquialisms are quite…. old, to be frank.

Written on the postcard are details that need no transcription, as they are simply address details that we have no business knowing. All you need to know are the following:

     To: ‘J.G.’
    From: ‘Holden H.V.’

That’s what it says on the mailing details. Next, on the actual contents of said letter, are the following:



                                 
















October 28, 984[1]

hello[2] , this is holden. that psychoanalyst guy[3] told me to write to you, so, i thought i would. I’m not gonna tell you my entire goddamn life story like robinson cruesoe[4] or whatever, but i’ll at least tell you a bit about my day as a fixer of the tri-axe office, in h. corp. oh yeah i moved here about ten months ago by the way. let me tell you, it’s been nothing short of a pain in the ass. seriously. every day, it’s either old shan-san[5] telling you to stick a finger up your crazy ass, or just plain nothing. he thought he was a goddamn hotshot, i’ll tell you. i mean, i get why, he’s like the third son or whatever the hell that’s supposed to mean, but still. there isn’t much else that’s bad in here. the food’s pretty solid, and there’s a bunch of neon signs, which usually bother me, but after being in t. corp , it’s a lot better to look at. i bet you’d like it. anyways, i wrote this letter to ask how you’ve been. i’d give you a call and all, but it’s either you’re busy, or im busy and i dont want to bother anybody to be completely honest. so, yeah. I[6] hope you’re doing well, Jane[7]. I hope you write back now. Please do so when you see this.

With much regardless[8],

Holden V.C.[9]

[1] As of writing this transcription, it is February 11th, 985. This means this was written quite a while ago.

[2] Throughout the whole letter, there is a very clear lack of proper grammar and punctuation. This implies that he was A. writing this casually, or B. wrote this nervous. His handwriting also indicates this.

[3] We have no idea who this is referring to.

[4]  He has misspelled “Robinson Crusoe", who is the protagonist of a popular fixer novel of the same name, where a Devyat Assoc. Fixer gets stuck on an island, and becomes king of said island.

[5] Obviously, you know who this is.

[6] Only after this specific part does he put in effort to make his handwriting look nice. Additionally, the rest of the letter, despite it only being a small part, is properly grammared and punctuated.

[7] The woman he was writing to.

[8] This is not a typo, he actually did write “regardless”.

[9] His full name is “Holden Morrissey Caulfield”, which the middle name certainly does not start with an V.
